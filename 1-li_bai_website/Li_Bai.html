李白
Li Bai

Li Bai (701-762), considered one of China's greatest poets alongside Du Fu, lived during the Tang Dynasty's golden age of poetry. Known for his vivid imagery and deep emotion, Li Bai's work spans about 1,000 poems on themes like nature, friendship, and existential reflections. Celebrated for his spontaneous verse and exploration of Taoist ideals, Li Bai's poetry also deeply admires the natural world and ancient times, often challenging life's ordinary constraints.

Famous Poems of Li Bai

Quiet Night Thoughts

Introduction: "Quiet Night Thoughts" by Li Bai first appeared in the year 726, in a collection titled The Complete Works of Li Po, translated by Sam Hamill. This short yet evocative poem captures the essence of homesickness and longing.

English Translation:

A pool of moonlight on my bed in this late hour
like a blanket of frost on the world.

I lift my eyes to a bright mountain moon.
Remembering my home, I bow.

- Li Po (translated by Sam Hamill)

Original Chinese:

静夜思

床前明月光，
疑是地上霜。
举头望明月，
低头思故乡。

The Hard Roads in Shu

Introduction: "Hard Roads in Shu" is an ancient Yuefu theme that literati throughout history have used to depict the treacherous paths to Sichuan, though their specific geographical references varied.

English Translation:

Oh, but it is high and very dangerous!
Such travelling is harder than scaling the blue sky.
...Until two rulers of this region
Pushed their way through in the misty ages,
Forty-eight thousand years had passed
With nobody arriving across the Ch'in border.

Original Chinese:

蜀道难

噫吁嚱，危乎高哉！
蜀道之难，难于上青天！
蚕丛及鱼凫，开国何茫然！
尔来四万八千岁，不与秦塞通人烟。

Drinking Alone Under the Moon

Introduction: This poem was written about the third year of Emperor Xuanzong's Tianbao (744), when Li Bai was in Chang'an, at a time when he was frustrated in officialdom.

English Translation:

Amidst the blooms, a jug of wine I pour,
A solitary drinker, forever more.
With cup aloft, I toast the silvery moon,
And with my shadow, a trio, we swoon.

Original Chinese:

月下獨酌

花間一壺酒，獨酌無相親。
舉杯邀明月，對影成三人。

Bring in the Wine

Introduction: This is one of Li Bai's most famous poems about wine and the celebration of life. Written during his later years, it reflects his Taoist philosophy of living in the moment.

English Translation:

See how the Yellow River's waters move out of heaven,
Entering the ocean, never to return.
See how lovely locks in bright mirrors in high chambers,
Though silken-black at morning, have changed by night to snow.

Original Chinese:

将进酒

君不见黄河之水天上来，奔流到海不复回。
君不见高堂明镜悲白发，朝如青丝暮成雪。

Farewell to a Friend

Introduction: This poem was written when Li Bai was saying goodbye to his friend Meng Haoran, who was leaving for Yangzhou.

English Translation:

At Yellow Crane Tower in the west
My old friend says farewell;
In the mist and flowers of spring
He goes down to Yangzhou.

Original Chinese:

黄鹤楼送孟浩然之广陵

故人西辞黄鹤楼，
烟花三月下扬州。
