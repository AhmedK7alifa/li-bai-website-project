Life of Li Bai

The Gifted Child (701-723)

During this stage, Li Bai demonstrated his talents through many writings and poems from a young age.

The Journey Abroad (724-729)

At the age of 24, carrying his sword, Li Bai left his hometown and began his journey in the world. These years blended his accumulated knowledge with real-world experience. It was during this time that he met his dear friend and idol, Meng Haoran.

The Wandering (730-733)

These were the years he searched for his place in society but failed to attain his desired outcome, leading to deeper self-reflection and poetic exploration.

The Ascending (734-741)

At age 33, he pursued recognition by submitting works to the royal court and forming connections among the aristocracy.

The Triumph (742-743)

Li Bai's work gained the admiration of the emperor, and he was invited to serve in the palace. Despite favor from the emperor, he grew weary of court life and was eventually dismissed after one year.

The Brotherhood with Du Fu

He formed a strong bond with the poet Du Fu. During their time together, they sang, wrote, traveled, and explored the world. They parted ways in 745—never to meet again.

The Trials of War

During the An Lushan Rebellion (755-763), he fled south, was captured, and escaped. These experiences added darker tones to his poetry and shaped a new style in his writing.

The End Scene

In the final chapter of his life, he handed his last poem to a friend. It was titled "The End Scene":

The mighty roc takes wing and soars,
Through all the realms, his strength endures.
He shakes the heavens with his might,
And in the wind, his spirit takes flight.

To the East he journeys on and on,
With my sleeve grasped tight, he is gone.
Let future generations hear my tale,
Of Confucius' passing, let none be pale.

For though he's gone, his legacy will live,
Through those inspired, his spirit will give.
Who will grieve and weep for my death,
When all that's left is my final breath?
